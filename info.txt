Toyota started in 1937 as a spinoff from a loom manufacturing company founded by Sakichi Toyoda, and it grew into one of the largest automakers in the world, famous for its “Toyota Production System,” which inspired modern lean manufacturing. The company’s name was changed from “Toyoda” to “Toyota” partly because it takes eight brush strokes to write in Japanese—considered a lucky number. Toyota produced the world’s best-selling car, the Toyota Corolla, with over 50 million units sold globally, and it was also a pioneer in hybrid technology with the Toyota Prius launched in 1997. Interestingly, Toyota once made luxury boats and even experimented with humanoid robots, and it still holds the record for producing a car every few seconds at peak efficiency across its global factories.
