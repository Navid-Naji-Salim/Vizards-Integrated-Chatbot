Toyota Motor Corporation, founded in 1937 by Kiichiro Toyoda, is one of the world’s largest automotive companies, headquartered in Toyota City, Japan. With a global presence in over 170 countries, Toyota has become synonymous with innovation and reliability, producing iconic models like the Corolla, Camry, and Land Cruiser. It pioneered hybrid technology with the Prius in 1997 and continues to lead in sustainable mobility with its hydrogen-powered Mirai and electric vehicles. Toyota’s production system, renowned for its lean manufacturing and the "Kaizen" philosophy of continuous improvement, has reshaped global manufacturing practices. The company is also investing heavily in sustainable initiatives, aiming for carbon neutrality by 2050. Toyota’s luxury division, Lexus, and its bold Woven City project, a smart city for testing futuristic technologies, further showcase its forward-thinking approach. With over 370,000 employees and annual revenues exceeding $275 billion, Toyota continues to be a dominant force in the global automotive industry.
